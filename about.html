About Me

I am a Machine Learning and Geospatial Researcher interested in building intelligent systems that connect simulation, computer vision, and large language models. My work focuses on how AI can learn from structured environmental data and simulated worlds to improve decision-making and perception in complex, real-world systems.

My research journey began during my MSc in Environmental Science (Data Science Specialization) at Thompson Rivers University, where I developed metaheuristic and spiral-based optimization algorithms for wildfire prediction across Canadian provinces. This work advanced feature selection in imbalanced datasets and contributed to the understanding of how AI can model spatial and temporal risk patterns.

Currently, I am exploring how simulation-driven learning, vision-based perception, and language-guided reasoning can converge to create adaptive and interpretable AI systems. I aim to continue this trajectory through a PhD or MSc-PhD program in Computer Science or Artificial Intelligence, focusing on multi-fidelity simulation, vision-language models, and generative world modeling for autonomous and scientific applications.
